# Compliance Framework Overview

## Purpose

This document outlines the compliance framework for grant management systems, focusing on alignment with US federal, state, and NGO requirements. The framework is designed to support mobile-first, cloud-agnostic implementations while maintaining rigorous compliance standards.

## Regulatory Landscape

### Federal Requirements

#### 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Administrative Requirements)
- **Financial Management**: Standards for accounting systems
- **Procurement**: Competitive bidding and documentation
- **Property Management**: Equipment tracking and disposition
- **Program Income**: Treatment of earned revenue

#### OMB Circulars
- **A-133**: Audit requirements for federal awards
- **A-110**: Administrative requirements for grants
- **A-102**: Common rule for state and local governments

#### Specific Agency Requirements
- **NIH**: Public Health Service policies
- **NSF**: Research grant administration
- **DOE**: Energy program compliance
- **DOL**: Workforce development standards

### State-Level Requirements

#### Common Elements
- **Single Audit Compliance**: State-specific extensions of federal requirements
- **Reporting Deadlines**: Varying by state and program
- **Documentation Standards**: State-prescribed formats
- **Monitoring Requirements**: Site visit and desk review protocols

#### Notable Variations
- **California**: Additional fair labor standards
- **New York**: Enhanced procurement requirements
- **Texas**: Specific documentation retention rules
- **Florida**: Unique reporting timelines

### NGO and Foundation Requirements

#### Private Foundation Standards
- **IRS Regulations**: 501(c)(3) compliance
- **Programmatic Reporting**: Outcome measurement expectations
- **Financial Transparency**: Public disclosure requirements
- **Conflict of Interest**: Board and staff regulations

#### International NGO Standards
- **OECD DAC**: Development assistance criteria
- **UN Sustainable Development Goals**: Alignment frameworks
- **IFI Requirements**: World Bank, regional development banks

## Compliance Pillars

### 1. Financial Management

#### Accounting Systems
- **Fund Accounting**: Separation by source and restriction
- **Cost Allocation**: Proper assignment of direct and indirect costs
- **Timekeeping**: Labor distribution documentation
- **Cash Management**: Drawdown timing and reconciliation

#### Internal Controls
- **Segregation of Duties**: Authorization, recording, and custody separation
- **Approval Hierarchies**: Multi-level approval requirements
- **Reconciliation Procedures**: Bank, ledger, and grant balance alignment
- **Documentation Standards**: Complete audit trail maintenance

### 2. Program Management

#### Performance Measurement
- **Outcome Tracking**: Quantitative and qualitative metrics
- **Milestone Documentation**: Evidence of progress achievement
- **Beneficiary Verification**: Participant eligibility confirmation
- **Geographic Compliance**: Service area restrictions

#### Monitoring and Evaluation
- **Site Visit Protocols**: Preparation and documentation
- **Desk Review Procedures**: Document submission and review
- **Corrective Action Plans**: Deficiency resolution tracking
- **Continuous Improvement**: Process refinement based on findings

### 3. Administrative Compliance

#### Personnel Management
- **Position Descriptions**: Role alignment with grant requirements
- **Time and Effort Reporting**: Federal standard compliance
- **Fringe Benefit Allocation**: Proper cost assignment
- **Training Documentation**: Staff competency verification

#### Procurement Standards
- **Competitive Bidding**: Documentation of selection processes
- **Vendor Qualifications**: Due diligence and vetting
- **Contract Administration**: Performance monitoring and payment
- **Conflict of Interest**: Disclosure and mitigation

## Implementation Framework

### Mobile-First Considerations

#### Accessibility Requirements
- **Section 508 Compliance**: Federal accessibility standards
- **WCAG 2.1 AA**: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
- **Mobile Optimization**: Responsive design for field use
- **Offline Capability**: Data capture without continuous connectivity

#### Security Protocols
- **Data Encryption**: At rest and in transit protection
- **Authentication**: Multi-factor verification options
- **Session Management**: Timeout and re-authentication
- **Device Management**: Lost device protocols

### Cloud-Agnostic Architecture

#### Data Portability
- **Standard Formats**: JSON, CSV, PDF export capabilities
- **API Access**: RESTful interfaces for system integration
- **Backup Procedures**: Regular, verifiable data exports
- **Migration Paths**: Clear procedures for platform changes

#### Compliance Documentation
- **Configuration Management**: Version-controlled settings
- **Change Logs**: Auditable modification records
- **Access Reports**: User activity monitoring
- **System Documentation**: Architecture and operation guides

## Compliance Verification

### Self-Assessment Tools
- **Checklists**: Regulatory requirement verification
- **Questionnaires**: Compliance status evaluation
- **Gap Analysis**: Deficiency identification and prioritization
- **Action Planning**: Remediation timeline development

### External Validation
- **Audit Preparation**: Document organization and review
- **Monitor Visits**: Site preparation and hosting
- **Regulatory Reviews**: Agency examination support
- **Accreditation**: Standards body certification

## Maintenance and Updates

### Regulatory Monitoring
- **Change Tracking**: Legislative and regulatory updates
- **Impact Analysis**: Requirement change implications
- **Implementation Planning**: Rollout of new requirements
- **Training Updates**: Staff education on changes

### Framework Evolution
- **Annual Review**: Comprehensive framework assessment
- **Stakeholder Feedback**: User experience incorporation
- **Best Practices Integration**: Industry standard adoption
- **Technology Adaptation**: New platform capability utilization
