# Data Retention and Archival Policy

## Purpose

This document establishes standardized data retention periods and archival procedures for grant management documentation. The policy aligns with federal, state, and organizational requirements while ensuring accessibility and security of historical records.

## Regulatory Framework

### Federal Requirements

#### 2 CFR Part 200.334 Retention Requirements
- **General Rule**: Records must be retained for 3 years from submission of final expenditure report
- **Exceptions**:
  - Litigation, claim, or audit findings: retain until resolved
  - Real property and equipment records: retain for 3 years after final disposition
  - Records for income transactions after period of performance: retain for 3 years

#### Specific Agency Extensions
- **National Institutes of Health**: 3 years after closeout, or longer if specified
- **National Science Foundation**: 3 years after submission of final expenditure report
- **Department of Education**: Generally 3 years, but varies by program
- **Department of Labor**: 3 years, with wage records requiring longer retention

#### OMB Circular A-110
- **Financial Records**: 3 years from submission of final expenditure report
- **Supporting Documents**: Must be retained with financial records
- **Property Records**: 3 years after final disposition

### State Requirements

#### Common State Standards
- **Financial Records**: Typically 3-7 years depending on state
- **Personnel Records**: Often 3-5 years after separation
- **Contract Records**: Usually duration plus 3-7 years
- **Tax Records**: Generally 3-4 years

#### Notable State Variations
- **California**: Up to 4 years for many records, longer for specific programs
- **New York**: 6 years for many financial records
- **Texas**: 3 years minimum, with extensions for certain programs
- **Florida**: 5 years for most state grant records

### Organizational Requirements

#### Internal Policies
- **Board Requirements**: May exceed regulatory minimums
- **Donor Expectations**: Specific foundation or individual requirements
- **Historical Preservation**: Organizational memory and continuity
- **Best Practices**: Industry standard adherence

## Retention Periods by Record Type

### Financial Records

#### Core Financial Documentation
- **General Ledger**: 7 years after fiscal year end
- **Accounts Payable**: 7 years after payment
- **Accounts Receivable**: 7 years after collection
- **Payroll Records**: 7 years after creation

#### Grant-Specific Financial Records
- **Expenditure Reports**: 7 years after submission
- **Drawdown Requests**: 7 years after transaction
- **Match Documentation**: 7 years after grant close
- **Program Income Records**: 7 years after generation

### Program Records

#### Participant Documentation
- **Eligibility Verification**: 3 years after service completion
- **Service Records**: 3 years after service delivery
- **Outcome Data**: 7 years after grant close
- **Case Notes**: 3 years after case closure

#### Performance Documentation
- **Progress Reports**: 7 years after grant close
- **Monitoring Reports**: 7 years after issuance
- **Evaluation Studies**: Permanent retention
- **Quality Assurance Records**: 3 years after creation

### Administrative Records

#### Personnel Documentation
- **Employment Applications**: 3 years after hiring decision
- **Personnel Files**: 7 years after separation
- **Time and Effort Reports**: 3 years after certification
- **Training Records**: 3 years after training completion

#### Procurement Documentation
- **Solicitations**: 3 years after contract completion
- **Contracts**: 7 years after expiration
- **Vendor Files**: 3 years after relationship termination
- **Bid Evaluations**: 3 years after award

### Compliance Records

#### Regulatory Documentation
- **Audit Reports**: Permanent retention
- **Compliance Certifications**: 7 years after submission
- **Policy Manuals**: Permanent retention of superseded versions
- **Exception Approvals**: 7 years after grant close

#### Communication Records
- **Grantor Correspondence**: 7 years after grant close
- **Internal Memoranda**: 3 years after creation
- **Meeting Minutes**: Permanent retention
- **Decision Documentation**: 7 years after decision

## Archival Procedures

### Record Classification

#### Retention Tier System
- **Tier 1 (Permanent)**: Historical, legal, or significant organizational value
- **Tier 2 (Long-term)**: 7+ year retention requirements
- **Tier 3 (Medium-term)**: 3-7 year retention requirements
- **Tier 4 (Short-term)**: Less than 3 year retention

#### Access Classification
- **Public Records**: Available for public review
- **Internal Records**: Organizational access only
- **Confidential Records**: Limited access with authorization
- **Restricted Records**: Highly sensitive, special handling required

### Digital Archival Standards

#### File Format Standards
- **Documents**: PDF/A for long-term preservation
- **Spreadsheets**: CSV or PDF for data preservation
- **Databases**: SQL dump with schema documentation
- **Email**: MBOX or PST with indexing

#### Metadata Requirements
- **Descriptive Metadata**: Title, creator, date, description
- **Administrative Metadata**: Rights, retention period, access controls
- **Technical Metadata**: File format, size, creation software
- **Preservation Metadata**: Checksums, migration history

### Physical Archival Standards

#### Storage Environment
- **Temperature**: 65-70°F (18-21°C)
- **Humidity**: 35-50% relative humidity
- **Light Control**: Limited exposure to ultraviolet light
- **Security**: Controlled access and fire protection

#### Organization System
- **Indexing**: Comprehensive finding aid development
- **Container Labeling**: Clear, consistent identification
- **Location Tracking**: System for retrieval and return
- **Condition Monitoring**: Regular preservation assessment

## Destruction Procedures

### Authorization Process

#### Review and Approval
- **Retention Review**: Verification of expiration date
- **Legal Review**: Confirmation of no legal holds
- **Management Approval**: Authorized destruction approval
- **Documentation**: Record of destruction maintained

#### Notification Requirements
- **Internal Notification**: Department heads informed
- **Regulatory Notification**: If required by specific programs
- **Donor Notification**: If specified in grant agreements
- **Public Notification**: For records subject to public records laws

### Destruction Methods

#### Digital Destruction
- **Secure Deletion**: Multiple overwrite standards (DoD 5220.22-M)
- **Media Destruction**: Physical destruction of storage media
- **Cloud Data**: Provider-certified destruction procedures
- **Verification**: Independent verification of destruction

#### Physical Destruction
- **Shredding**: Cross-cut or micro-cut standards
- **Pulping**: For large volume paper destruction
- **Incineration**: Secure facility with emission controls
- **Recycling**: When security allows, with certified vendors

### Documentation of Destruction

#### Record Keeping
- **Destruction Log**: Date, method, and volume record
- **Certificate of Destruction**: Vendor certification
- **Approval Documentation**: Authorization records
- **Exception Documentation**: Records retained beyond period

#### Audit Trail
- **Retention Period**: Original retention requirement
- **Review Date**: When destruction was authorized
- **Method Verification**: How destruction was verified
- **Responsible Party**: Who authorized and executed

## Special Considerations

### Litigation Holds

#### Implementation
- **Identification**: Records subject to legal action
- **Notification**: All relevant personnel informed
- **Suspension**: Destruction procedures halted
- **Preservation**: Enhanced protection implemented

#### Management
- **Scope Definition**: Clear parameters for hold
- **Communication**: Regular updates to affected parties
- **Documentation**: Comprehensive hold records
- **Release**: Formal termination when resolved

### Electronic Records Management

#### System Requirements
- **Version Control**: Track changes and revisions
- **Access Logs**: Record all access and modifications
- **Export Capability**: Standard format data extraction
- **Search Functionality**: Comprehensive retrieval tools

#### Migration Planning
- **Format Migration**: Periodic update to current formats
- **System Migration**: Transfer between platforms
- **Verification**: Data integrity confirmation
- **Documentation**: Migration process and results

### Privacy and Security

#### Protected Information
- **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)**: Enhanced protection required
- **Health Information**: HIPAA compliance for PHI
- **Financial Information**: Protection of account and transaction data
- **Proprietary Information**: Confidential business information

#### Security Measures
- **Encryption**: At rest and in transit protection
- **Access Controls**: Role-based permission systems
- **Audit Trails**: Comprehensive access logging
- **Incident Response**: Breach detection and response plans
